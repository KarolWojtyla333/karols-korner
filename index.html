Who Am I?

I was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada in 1999 to parents of Salvadoran and Polish descent. Some people know Winnipeg for its famously windy intersections, its title as the Slurpee Capital of the World, or simply as one of the coldest cities around — but for me, it was home. I spent the first five years of my life there, surrounded by family and a vibrant, diverse community of friends, before we made the move to Austin, Texas. From an early age, I was surrounded by curiosity. My dad and two half-sisters were always tinkering — fixing things, breaking them down, putting them back together, and sharing knowledge like it was second nature. Their love for science, electronics, and figuring out how things worked sparked something in me. They laid the foundation for my interest in the STEM field, and it wasn't long before I was doing the same: reverse-engineering things, rebuilding them, and sometimes even making them better. But it wasn’t just the technical side that shaped me. My mom's compassion and open-hearted nature taught me to value people just as much as problem-solving. Her kindness showed me how important it is to listen, to connect, and to always be curious about others — not just about how things work, but how people think, feel, and live. Between both sides of my family, I was raised to be open, outgoing, and unafraid of discomfort if it meant growing. Whether it’s stepping into a new environment, trying something I’ve never done before, or learning from someone with a completely different perspective — I’m always eager to dive in.
